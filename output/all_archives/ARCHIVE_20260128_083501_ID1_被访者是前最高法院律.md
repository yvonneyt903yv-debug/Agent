## 【中文全译文】
# 掌控人生的实用指南：凯特·霍尔（文字稿）

**编辑注**：前最高法院律师、顶尖扑克选手凯特·霍尔分享了她如何从药物成瘾、几乎失去一切的困境，转变为领导一家价值数十亿美元科技基金会的经历。她提出，有意义人生的真正引擎是「个人能动性」——即发现并利用隐藏选项的能力，并提供了「主动寻求拒绝」、获取残酷诚实反馈等具体策略，帮助人们拓展自由空间，无论当下感到多么束手无策。（2025年8月28日）

**文字稿**

## 从成瘾到能动性

**凯特·霍尔**：

五年前，我曾是自己人生的囚徒。我深陷药物成瘾无法自拔。每天清晨醒来，我会先去购买药物，然后浑浑噩噩地使用一整天，直到深夜再次昏迷。这样的日子持续了数月。那段记忆大多模糊，但我清晰记得一种强烈的震撼与愤懑——看着普通人做着寻常小事时，那种心情难以言喻。目睹有人与朋友共进午餐，我会觉得难以置信：怎么会有人能如此自由地决定下午的安排？

本次分享的重点并非成瘾本身，但我想让你们先理解我的起点，明白我曾多么困顿，再告诉你们我现在的人生何等精彩。首先，我已彻底戒除毒瘾；其次，我与一位非凡的男子结婚，共同从事各种有趣的项目；此外，我担任Astera研究院的CEO，这家拥有数十亿美元资金的私人基金会正开创支持科技创新的新模式。

## 个人能动性的力量

今天我想重点探讨的是如何从A点抵达B点。改变的关键是什么？并非我变得更聪明或更努力，而是更根本的转变——我培养起了个人能动性。我将这种能力理解为：既能洞察我们实际拥有的自由度，又能据此采取行动。它关乎发现生活围墙中隐藏的门径。

我认为，对于追求充实有意义的人生而言，能动性远比我们通常看重的智力、勤奋等成功要素更重要。这些要素若被误用便毫无价值，且随着机器逐渐取代人类执行这些功能，其重要性正日益减弱。Y Combinator首席执行官盖瑞·谭近期有句令我深有共鸣的话：「智力已可随时调用，能动性因而愈发关键。」

## 绝望的馈赠

尽管成瘾剥夺了我的自由，但它却赋予我培养能动性的特殊优势。因为能动性有诸多催化剂，绝望无疑是其中之一——成瘾者称之为「绝望的馈赠」，即不惜一切改变生活的决心。无论是站在满屋陌生人面前羞耻地说「我叫凯特，我是个瘾君子」，还是将自己封闭数月，或是服用那些饮酒就会进急诊室的药物。

进入康复中心时，我已充分获得这份「馈赠」：工作丢了，朋友散了，一度连行走能力都丧失。离开时，我踏入过渡教习所，面对一团乱麻的生活。但某种程度上，这反而是好事。因为一无所有，让我变得无畏而饥渴。

## 对一切机会说「是」

我开始接纳所有可能性。只要有人愿意牵线，无论看似能否助我重整旗鼓，我都全力尝试。我记得自己当时追求的是数量而非精准判断每件事的收益。正是这种态度让我最终……

在过去的四年里，我几乎和所有共事过的人道了歉。

失去自尊心也让我学得更快。我的大脑曾受过损伤，这意味着我并不总能理解别人说的话。而且我也无法假装听懂了。因此我变得很擅长说：“我没听懂您刚才说的，您能解释一下吗？”——而在以前类似的情况下，我可能只会点头附和。顺便说一句，人们其实很乐意解释事情。这完全是双赢。

现在我要告诉大家一个好消息：你不需要先毁掉自己的生活再重建它，也能学会如何更真实地活着。我确实认为某种程度的绝望感会有所帮助。但生活中总有些事情会让人感到绝望。

疫情期间，当我看到低收入国家因缺乏足够的冷链存储而难以推广疫苗接种时，我和朋友们感受到了这种绝望。于是我们创立了一家公司，研发出了可在常温下保存的疫苗，正是这种绝望感推动我们在不到六个月的时间内进入临床试验阶段，速度超过了历史上任何一家初创公司。

在我婚姻的早期，我也感受到另一种绝望——当时我们两人之间仿佛隔着一堵无形的墙。于是在绝望中，我学会了如何化解那些阻碍我与他人建立联系的情感隔阂。

我并不认为主观能动性是天生的，但我相信大多数人都是凭借纯粹的运气才学会了它。如果不是绝望带来的运气，那可能就是近距离观察高度自主的人如何行事所带来的运气。不过我也认为，这种能力可以通过系统性的学习被更多人掌握。

## 提升主观能动性的实用方法

我想分享一些我学到的提升主观能动性的方法。

第一，假定所有事物都是可以学习的。

第二，主动寻求拒绝。我们终其一生都在小心规避被拒绝，但如果你只追求触手可及的目标，实际上是在委屈自己。事实上，有时你需要瞄准那些看似不合理的目标，才能验证自己对"合理"的判断是否准确。上次我求职时，曾对几个人说过："我正考虑创办一个类似你们这样的机构。不如让我来管理你们的机构如何？"这听起来可能有点妄想，但关键是——有时妄想确实能成真。

第三，寻求真实反馈。我们几乎每个人都被某些自己完全意识不到、但他人一目了然的因素所束缚。你难道不想知道那是什么吗？最好的发现方法就是让人们能够匿名告诉你。我知道这听起来可能有些可怕——最初我也这么觉得——但这也会令人振奋。

我的推特个人资料里设置了一个匿名反馈箱，说实话这改变了我的生活。不仅是因为收到的具体反馈，更重要的是我意识到自己不再试图逃避真相。

## 主观能动性的力量

如果我能回到五年前，告诉当时的自己：有一天我会体验到那种自由——无需隐藏秘密，可以随意支配每个下午，基本处于快乐的状态——我绝不会相信。

但这就是主观能动性的力量。无论你感到多么困顿，只要学会发现内心隐藏的门扉，就能开启超乎想象的自由。

谢谢。

## 【英文原文】
# A Practical Guide to Taking Control of Your Life: Cate Hall (Transcript)

Editor’s Notes: Former Supreme Court attorney and top-ranked poker player Cate Hall shares how she went from drug addiction and losing almost everything to leading a multibillion-dollar science and technology foundation. She argues that the real engine of a meaningful life is personal agency — the skill of spotting and using hidden options — and offers concrete tactics like “courting rejection” and seeking brutally honest feedback to expand your freedom, no matter how stuck you feel. (Aug 28, 2025)

TRANSCRIPT:

## From Addiction to Agency

CATE HALL:

Five years ago, I was a prisoner in my own life. I was hopelessly addicted to drugs. Every morning I would get up, go buy drugs, and then spend the rest of the day using, barely conscious, until I passed out again at the end of the night. I spent months at a time like that. I don’t have a lot of memories from that time, but one thing I do remember very clearly is this incredible sense of awe and resentment I felt just watching normal people do normal things. I would see somebody meeting a friend for lunch, and it would seem inconceivable to me that anybody could be that free, that they could just decide what to do with an afternoon.

This talk isn’t about addiction per se, but I’m telling you this because I really need you to understand where I’m coming from, how trapped I was, before I tell you that my life is amazing now. I’m clean, first and foremost. I’m married to an incredible man, and we get to do all sorts of fun projects together. And I’m CEO of Astera Institute, a multi-billion dollar private foundation that’s pioneering a new approach to supporting innovative science and technology.

## The Power of Personal Agency

What I do want to talk about today is how I got from point A to point B. What changed? It’s not that I got smarter, or that I started trying harder. I think what changed was even more fundamental. It was developing a sense of personal agency, which I think about as the capacity to both see and act on all of the degrees of freedom we actually have. It’s about being able to find the hidden doors in the walls of life.

I want to argue that when it comes to living a satisfying and meaningful life, agency is actually much more important than the things we usually think about as critical to success, like intelligence and hard work, both of which are next to useless if misapplied, and which are becoming less and less important as we increasingly outsource them to machines. I saw a quote recently from Gary Tan, the CEO of Y Combinator, that I really liked. He said, “Intelligence is on tap now, so agency is even more important.”

## The Gift of Desperation

For all of the freedom that addiction took from me, I think it actually gave me an unnatural advantage when it came to cultivating agency. And that’s because, while agency has many mothers, one of them is certainly desperation. Addicts call this “the gift of desperation,” actually. The willingness to do whatever it takes to change your life. To embarrass yourself by standing up in front of a room full of strangers and saying, “My name is Kate and I’m a drug addict.” Or to lock yourself away for months. Or to take medications that will put you in the ER if you drink.

By the time I went to rehab, I definitely had the gift of desperation. I’d lost my job, most of my friends. For a time, I’d basically lost the ability to walk. And so when I left, I walked into a halfway house and a complete mess of a life. But in a way, I think that was actually good. Because I felt like I had nothing left to lose. And that made me fearless and hungry.

## Saying Yes to Everything

I started saying yes to everything. Every connection someone was willing to make in hopes it might lead to something that would help me get back on my feet. I remember just going for volume. It didn’t matter if I could tell how something would benefit me. That’s how I ended up meeting most of the people I’ve worked with in the last four years.

Losing my sense of pride also helped me learn really fast. I had brain damage, which meant that I didn’t always understand things. And I couldn’t pretend that I did, either. So I got good at saying, “I don’t understand what you just said. Can you explain it to me?” In situations where before, I might have just nodded along. Side note, people love to explain things. The total win-win.

Now, I have great news, which is that you don’t need to ruin your life and then rebuild it in order to learn to be more authentic. I do think it helps to be some kind of desperate. But there’s always something to be desperate for.

I felt that during COVID, as friends and I watched low-income countries struggle with vaccinations because they lacked adequate cold chain storage. So we created a company that created a shelf-stable vaccine, and we let that desperation drive us into clinical trials in under six months, faster than any startup in history.

I felt another kind of desperation early on in my marriage, when it seemed like there was an invisible wall between the two of us. So in desperation, I learned how to resolve the emotional barriers that made it difficult for me to connect with people.

I don’t think agency is innate, but I do think most people learn it through sheer luck. If it’s not the luck of desperation, then maybe it’s just the luck of seeing somebody highly agentic operating up close. I also think, though, that it can be learned systematically and by many more people.

## Tactics for Becoming More Agentic

I want to share some of the tactics I’ve learned for becoming more agentic.

First, assume everything is learnable.

Second, court rejection. We spend our lives carefully avoiding it, but if you’re only aiming for things you get, you’re doing yourself a disservice. In fact, sometimes you have to aim for things that feel unreasonable to make sure your instinct about what’s reasonable is right. Last time I was applying for a job, I told a couple people, “I’m thinking about starting an organization much like your own. Can I run yours instead?” A little delusional, maybe, but the thing is, sometimes delusional works.

Third, seek real feedback. Pretty much every one of us has something holding us back that we’re completely blind to and that’s obvious to other people. Don’t you want to know what that is? The single best way to find out is to give people a way to tell you anonymously. I know that might sound scary, it was to me at first, but it can also be exhilarating.

I have an anonymous feedback box linked to my Twitter profile and it has honestly been life-changing, not just in terms of the specific feedback I’ve gotten, but in knowing that I’m not trying to hide things from myself anymore.

## The Power of Personal Agency

If I could go back in time five years and talk to the person that I was then, and tell her that I would one day experience that kind of freedom, to not have to hide things, to do whatever I feel like with my afternoons, to be basically happy, I would not have believed it.

But that is the power of personal agency. No matter how stuck you are, if you can learn to locate the doors hidden within you, you can unlock inconceivable kinds of freedom.

Thank you.
